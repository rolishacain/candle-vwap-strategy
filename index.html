How This Strategy Works

The Inside Candle marks consolidation. Price breaks out of the IC range, then retests the IC high or low — that retest is your entry. VWAP across all timeframes provides the directional bias filter.

Inside Candle Level — the IC high and low become your key S/R levels after the breakout. You mark them, wait for the break, then wait for the retest of those same levels.
VWAP Filter — sets directional bias on all TFs. Price above VWAP = longs only. Below = shorts only. If TFs conflict, skip the trade.
The Retest Entry — price breaks the IC level, pulls back to retest it (now flipped S/R), and you enter on confirmation of the hold. Better fill, tighter stop, higher R:R than chasing the initial break.
The Edge — you only trade breakouts that are confirmed by VWAP direction, on multiple timeframes agreeing.
CORE PHILOSOPHY
01 — WAIT
Don't chase. The inside candle is your signal to prepare, not to act. Patience is the strategy.
02 — ALIGN
VWAP must agree with your direction across your key timeframes before you place an order.
03 — WAIT FOR RETEST
After the IC level breaks, do not enter immediately. Wait for price to pull back and retest the IC high/low. That retest is your entry signal.
04 — PROTECT
Stop goes behind the inside candle or mother candle. Minimum 2:1 reward-to-risk or don't take it.
YOUR ENTRY METHOD
Retest of the Inside Candle Level
You do not enter on the initial breakout of the IC. Instead: price breaks the IC high or low → you watch → price pulls back to retest that same level → level holds → you enter. This means every entry is at a confirmed flip of support or resistance, not a momentum chase.

Setup Identification

Mark the Inside Candle High & Low

On 1H or 15M, identify a clean inside candle fully contained within the mother. Draw horizontal lines at the IC high and IC low — these are your trigger levels. The IC should be near VWAP, not extended far from it. Multi-bar IC compression (2–3 bars) = stronger coil = more powerful retest.

Mark IC High
Mark IC Low

3
Watch for the Breakout

Let Price Break the IC Level First — Don't Enter Yet

Wait for a candle to close beyond the IC high (for longs) or IC low (for shorts). This is your signal to go on alert — not your entry. The breakout validates the setup. Now your only job is to wait for the retest of that same level.

Long: close above IC high
Short: close below IC low
Do NOT enter here
4
The Retest — Your Actual Entry

Enter When Price Returns to the IC Level and Holds

After the breakout, price pulls back to retest the IC high (now support) or IC low (now resistance). You enter when price touches and shows rejection from that level. Look for a rejection wick, a small bounce candle, or a VWAP hold on the lower TF to confirm the level is holding before placing your order.

LONG RETEST
Price breaks above IC high → pulls back to IC high → IC high holds as support → enter long on bounce confirmation
SHORT RETEST
Price breaks below IC low → pulls back to IC low → IC low holds as resistance → enter short on rejection confirmation
Rejection wick = best signal
VWAP holding = added confluence
5
Stop Loss Placement

Stop Goes Below/Above the IC Level You Just Retested

For longs: stop goes below the IC high (the level that just held as support) or below the IC low for a wider stop. For shorts: stop goes above the IC low. If price returns back through the IC level after you've entered, the retest has failed — get out. Do not give it room to "come back."

Long stop: below IC high retest
Short stop: above IC low retest
Tight stop = high R:R advantage
6
Targets & R:R

Define Targets Before You Enter

Target 1 (1.5R): Take partial profits — move stop to breakeven on remaining position. Target 2 (3R+): Trail the runner via VWAP on lower TF. Minimum 2:1 R:R or the trade doesn't meet your criteria — skip it and wait for the next retest setup.

T1 = 1.5R → breakeven stop
T2 = 3R+ → trail via VWAP
Min 2:1 or skip
7
Invalidation Rules

When the Retest Setup is Dead

No retest within 3–5 candles: momentum has moved on — the setup is stale, cancel your order. Retest level breaks: price closes back through the IC level from the entry side — exit immediately. VWAP flips: if VWAP sentiment changes direction during the retest wait period, the trade is off.

No retest in 3–5 bars = cancel
Level breaks = exit
VWAP flips = trade off

RETEST QUALITY GUIDE — WHAT TO LOOK FOR AT THE LEVEL
✅ STRONG RETEST
Rejection wick touches IC level then closes away · Quick 1–2 candle retest, no lingering · VWAP holding in your favor on LTF · High volume on the breakout candle, lower volume on the retest
⚠️ WEAK RETEST
Price grinds along IC level for many candles · Multiple closes above and below the level · VWAP is conflicting on LTF · Retest takes more than 5–6 candles to resolve
❌ FAILED RETEST
Price closes back through the IC level on the retest side · Setup is invalidated — do not enter or exit immediately if already in · Wait for a fresh IC to form before trading again
WHY THE RETEST ENTRY WINS
Entering on the retest of the IC level instead of the initial breakout gives you three structural advantages:
01 — BETTER PRICE
You enter closer to the actual S/R level, not in the middle of a momentum burst. This alone improves your average R:R across all trades.
02 — TIGHTER STOP
Your stop goes just below/above the IC level. Because you're entering at the level itself, the stop distance is minimal — often 2–5 ticks depending on the asset.
03 — CONFIRMATION
The level has been tested and is holding. You're not guessing — you're reacting to price proving the level matters. The market tells you the trade is on.

WHEN TO SKIP THE RETEST — INVALIDATION SCENARIOS
Retest takes too long
If price doesn't retest within 3–5 candles on the setup timeframe, the momentum has already moved on. Cancel your alert. Don't chase a stale retest.
Level completely blown through
Price comes back, closes back below the IC high (for longs) — the level isn't holding. This is a failed retest. Do not enter. Wait for a new IC to form.
VWAP flips during the wait
If VWAP sentiment turns against your bias while you're waiting for the retest, the setup context has changed. Skip it — the odds have shifted.
Retest with no rejection signal
Price drifts back to the level, sits there, no rejection wick, no clear bounce candle. Weak retest = weak odds. Only enter when the level shows it's defending itself.
